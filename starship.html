FAQ

Common questions

Starship is fully and rapidly reusable — like an airplane — which could reduce the cost of reaching orbit by 100x. This makes large-scale space exploration economically viable for the first time.
Super Heavy is the first stage of Starship. It carries 33 Raptor engines and is designed to be caught back at the launch tower by giant mechanical arms called "Mechazilla" or the "Chopstick arms".
SpaceX plans uncrewed cargo missions to Mars as early as the late 2020s, with crewed missions targeted for the early 2030s — though timelines in space are always optimistic.
Starship uses liquid methane (CH₄) and liquid oxygen (LOX). Methane was chosen because it can be produced on Mars from its atmosphere — enabling a return trip using local resources.
